Enrollment System Review

January 14, 2026

Using OES for the second time

Going into second semester, I got to experience the Online Enrollment System again, and honestly, I have a lot to say about it. It has been a long time since I first used the system to enroll myself in this university, and now that I am using it again, it made me stop and think: is there really anything to nitpick about it? That question came to mind because, overall, the system feels convenient for me and for many of my fellow students. As yanos, we usually just want things to work smoothly without too much effort, and in that sense, the Online Enrollment System does its job fairly well.

One thing that stood out to me immediately is how simple the system is. The simplicity of the Online Enrollment System feels like its main identity. It does not try to overwhelm students with too many buttons, features, or confusing layouts. Instead, it focuses on the essentials. This makes sense because students do not use the enrollment system often. Unlike the USEP Virtual Environment, which students regularly visit to check announcements, view grades, or access learning materials, the enrollment system is only used during specific times of the school year. Because of that, it does not need to be something students interact with daily. It just needs to be reliable and easy to understand when it mattersmost.

Personally, I see the Online Enrollment System as a window into the enrollment process. It gives students a general idea of what is happening, even if it does not explain everything in detail. When I submit my enrollment, I at least know that my information has been received and that the process is moving forward. Even though the system can feel vague at times, I would still prefer this over not knowing anything at all. Having some visibility into the process gives reassurance, especially for students who are anxious about deadlines and requirements.

However, this same vagueness can also cause confusion. There were moments when I questioned whether my submission had actually gone through or if I needed to redo certain steps. The system often does not give detailed confirmation messages, which can make students second-guess themselves. During enrollment periods, many students are already stressed due to time pressure, limited slots, or internet issues. A lack of clear feedback can add unnecessary anxiety. Even a simple confirmation message stating that everything was successfully submitted would greatly improve the experience.

Despite these concerns, I cannot deny that the system saves a lot of time and effort. Compared to traditional enrollment methods that require long lines and multiple campus visits, the online system is a huge improvement. Students can complete the process from home, at their own pace, as long as they have internet access. This is especially helpful for those who live far from the university or have other responsibilities to manage. For me, being able to enroll without physically going to campus is a big advantage.

Another positive aspect of the Online Enrollment System is that it reduces confusion caused by paperwork. Everything is centralized in one place, and students do not have to worry about losing forms or missing signatures. Having a digitalsystem also makes it easier to keep records for future reference. If there is ever a problem with enrollment, there is at least a digital trail that can be checked. This adds a layer of convenience that traditional systems lack.

That said, the system is not perfect. Some parts of the interface feel too plain, almost to the point where students are unsure what to do next. First-time users, in particular, may struggle with understanding the flow of the process. While returning students may already be familiar with the steps, new students could benefit from clearer instructions or short explanations on each page. The system assumes that users already know what to do, which is not always the case.

Another area that could be improved is how the system performs during peak enrollment times. When many students access the site at the same time, it can become slow or unresponsive and even crash like when i enrolled most of my classmatesare not enrolled yet. Pages take longer to load, and sometimes actions feel delayed. This can be frustrating for the instructors causing a lot of back log, especially when students are racing against time to secure their slots. While thisissue does not always happen, it becomes noticeable during busy periods and affects the overall experience.

The system’s mobile experience could also use some attention. Many students rely heavily on their phones for online tasks, including enrollment. While the system is accessible on mobile devices, some elements feel cramped or harder to interact with on smaller screens. Improving mobile usability would make the system more accessible and student-friendly, especially for those who do not always have access to a laptop or desktop computer.

Even with these issues, I still believe that the Online Enrollment System fulfills its main purpose. It is functional, relatively easy to use, and much more convenient than older enrollment methods. The system may not be perfect, but it shows that the university is making an effort to modernize its processes and adapt to the needs of students. With a few adjustments focused on clarity, feedback, and usability, the system could become even better.

As a student who has used the Online Enrollment System more than once, I can say that my overall experience has been positive. Each time I use it, I feel more familiar with the process, and that familiarity reduces stress. I appreciate the fact that the system does not overcomplicate things and sticks to what is necessary. While there are moments of confusion, they do not completely take away from the system’s usefulness.

In conclusion, the Online Enrollment System is a simple yet effective tool that plays an important role in the student experience. Its strength lies in its simplicity and convenience, making enrollment more accessible and less time-consuming. Although there are areas that need improvement, especially in terms of feedback and user guidance, the system is a step in the right direction. With continuous refinement, it can become a more reliable and reassuring platform for students in the future. For now, it does what it needs to do — and for most students, that is enough.
